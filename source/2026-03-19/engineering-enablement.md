# Engineering Enablement — Abi Noda
**Date:** Thu, Mar 19, 2026
**Subject:** Why aren't AI productivity gains higher?

## Summary
Research-backed analysis of why real-world AI productivity gains for developers are more modest than headlines suggest, with anonymous developer quotes.

Key findings:
1. **Coding isn't the main bottleneck** — Microsoft research puts coding at ~16% of a developer's time. Even halving code time has limited total impact.
2. **Partial SDLC automation creates new bottlenecks** — AI speeds up coding but creates downstream review/validation bottlenecks. "AI-generated code saved time is spent on extended reviews, fact checking or issue remediation, resulting in net-zero productivity gain."
3. **Social friction** — Polarization between pro/anti-AI engineers; unclear norms; isolated solo adopters can't materialize gains.
4. **Tooling and skill gaps** — Learning to "agentically code" is its own discipline; tools don't fit cleanly into existing workflows.
5. **Lack of business context** — AI lacks implicit knowledge: why systems were designed a certain way, business constraints, tacit rules.

Other observations: AI excels in greenfield projects; documentation quality is improving but can also be incorrect; concern about developers offloading critical thinking.
