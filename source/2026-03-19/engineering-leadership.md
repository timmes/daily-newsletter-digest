# Engineering Leadership — Gregor Ojstersek
**Date:** Thu, Mar 19, 2026
**Subject:** The "Spotlight Effect" Is Holding You Back as an Engineer or Engineering Leader

## Summary
The spotlight effect is a cognitive bias where we overestimate how much others notice us, think of us, or judge our actions. The article explores how this effect holds engineers and engineering leaders back from pursuing career growth — from applying for jobs, speaking up in meetings, starting a blog, or stepping into management.

Key points:
- Thomas Gilovich's experiment: participants thought ~50% of people noticed their embarrassing T-shirt; only ~20–25% actually did
- Author's personal examples: hesitation as a self-taught engineer, imposter syndrome as a senior engineer, fears as a new manager
- People are too busy with their own "spotlight" to focus on yours
- The solution: recognize the bias and take action despite the fear
