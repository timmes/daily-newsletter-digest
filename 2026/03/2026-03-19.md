# Daily Digest — 2026-03-19

## Top Stories

1. **Why aren't AI productivity gains higher?** *(Engineering Enablement — Abi Noda)*
   Developers explain why real-world AI gains are modest: coding is only ~16% of engineering time; AI speeds up code but creates downstream review bottlenecks; social friction between pro/anti-AI camps; and AI lacks the implicit business context that experienced engineers carry in their heads. The next wave of gains will come from the human parts of the SDLC.

2. **Three Claude Skills That Make You Harder to Replace** *(Age of Product — Stefan Wolpers)*
   Free download: three Claude `.skill` files that turn AI into a structured thinking partner — Socratic Explorer (understand before solving), Brutal Critic (stress-test your plan), Pre-Mortem (assume failure, find the tripwire). Research-backed: imagining failure increases reason-identification by 30%. Not about speed — about judgment.

3. **Part 1: I've Been a Platform PM for 6 Years — I'm Open-Sourcing Everything I Know** *(The Platform Space — Alex Craciun)*
   Alex Craciun built a CLAUDE.md knowledge base encoding 6 years of Platform PM expertise — frameworks, real outcomes (£1.6M ARR, 90% deployment time reduction), discovery templates, mental models. Building it forced her to make tacit knowledge explicit. Part 2 covers skills; Part 3 covers what to delegate vs. keep. MVP agent available now.

4. **Exploit vs Explore** *(Fish Food for Thought — Mike Fisher)*
   Bees and the multi-armed bandit problem as lenses on product leadership. Even when a hive finds a rich food source, scout bees keep exploring — insurance, not inefficiency. Product orgs that always exploit are optimising toward stagnation. Your roadmap is a portfolio; portfolios need diversification and a tolerance for uncertainty. Team topology is the structural lever.

5. **Get the Most Out of Your Performance Review** *(Level Up Newsletter — Ethan Evans)*
   11-step framework for owning your annual review (free portion: 4 steps). Key: clarity is the goal, not validation. Build a year of small feedback loops; recency bias is real. Own the context when managers change — don't wait and see. Align to motivating work early in the year.

6. **Claude Code for Absolute Beginners — April 14** *(Every — Mike Taylor)*
   Full-day live Zoom workshop for non-technical people who want to build with Claude Code. No programming experience required. Led by Mike Taylor + Every CEO Dan Shipper. Early bird $1,050 (through March 25). Past attendee shipped an internal dashboard in 7 hours.

7. **The "Spotlight Effect" Is Holding You Back** *(Engineering Leadership — Gregor Ojstersek)*
   The spotlight effect: we overestimate how much others notice or judge us. Only ~20–25% of people noticed an embarrassing T-shirt in Gilovich's experiment, vs. the 50% participants expected. Gregor shares personal examples from his career as a self-taught engineer and first-time manager. The fix: recognise the bias and act anyway.
