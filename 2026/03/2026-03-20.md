# Daily Newsletter Digest - 2026-03-20

## Top Stories

1. **How to Build an AI Style Guide** *(Every / Katie Parrott)*
   A step-by-step guide to teaching an LLM to write in your voice. Drawing on the history of Hart's Rules and the AP Stylebook, the piece argues that an AI style guide forces you to surface the subconscious patterns of your writing — and the person who learns most from writing it is you. [Read →](https://every.to/guides/ai-style-guide)

2. **AI Agent-Generated Pull Requests Are Breaking Open Source** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   AI agents are spamming large repos like Hugging Face's with dozens of useless PRs per hour. Mitchell Hashimoto's new tool *Vouch* requires contributors to be vouched for to stop the slop. The "anyone can open a PR" model may effectively be dead. [Read →](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/p/the-pulse-is-the-fde-role-becoming)

3. **Jack Dorsey: AI Models Drove Block's 40% Layoffs** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   Dorsey revealed that Anthropic's Opus 4.6 and OpenAI's Codex 5.3 becoming capable with large codebases in December was the trigger for restructuring Block. "We have to rethink how companies are built."

4. **OpenAI Acquires Astral — Creators of the `uv` Python Package Manager** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   OpenAI snapped up the team behind the blazing-fast Rust-written `uv` to join its Codex team — mirroring how Anthropic acquired Bun's creators for Claude Code. AI labs are buying world-class tooling expertise in their primary languages.

5. **FDE Role: High Demand, Low Desire** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   Forward Deployed Engineer job postings grew 10x on Indeed in 2025, yet most engineers don't want the role. Reality: it's closer to solutions engineering or consulting than product software dev. "Only maybe 10% of the market wants that role."

6. **Microsoft vs. OpenAI vs. AWS: The $50B Cloud Contract Fight** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   OpenAI's contract requires all API access to run on Azure. Its new $50B AWS partnership may breach this, and Microsoft is threatening legal action. "We know our contract. We will sue them if they breach it."

7. **Cursor Silent Price Hike Burns Enterprise Trust** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   Cursor moved all major models to "Max mode" on 16 March, consuming credits ~10x faster, without notice. Released Composer 2.0 the same day. Enterprise customers at Fortune 500s are furious. A trust-burning move from a $2B+ ARR company.

8. **Musk Fires More xAI Co-Founders Over Slow Coding Product** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   Elon Musk has pushed out two more xAI co-founders (Zihang Dai, Guodong Zhang) over poor performance of the coding product. Only 3 of the original 12 co-founders remain. SpaceX/Tesla "fixers" parachuted in to audit.

9. **Meta Shuts Down Horizon Worlds — Metaverse Dream Effectively Over** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
   Meta is closing its flagship VR social app in June. The name change from Facebook to "Meta" in 2021 looks increasingly like a costly detour.

10. **AWS Ends PostgreSQL 13 Support Without Full PG14 Coverage** *(The Pragmatic Engineer)*
    AWS is forcing RDS PostgreSQL 13 customers to upgrade immediately to PG14, but hasn't fully tested the upgrade path. A reliability stumble for a usually dependable cloud provider.
