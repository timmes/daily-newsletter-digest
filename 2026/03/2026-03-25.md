# Daily Newsletter Digest - 2026-03-25

## Top Stories

### 1. The Agent That Saved My Brain — *Every*
Every's head of growth Austin Tedesco built "Montaigne," a personal AI agent running in Claude Code, connected to Stripe, PostHog, Slack, Notion, Figma, and the company's full product suite. The agent has over 80 custom skills and lives both in his terminal and as an OpenClaw bot in Slack, where any teammate can tag it. Campaign briefs that used to take days of manual data assembly now take a voice note plus a few minutes. The deeper insight: Montaigne's value isn't speed — it's that Austin arrives at high-judgment decisions with energy to spare, because the cognitive load work has been offloaded. The one honest tension he surfaces is the seductive loop of improving the system instead of shipping; his fix is to ask Montaigne to knock out the to-do list one by one while spawning subagents in the background. The compound knowledge plugin is open-sourced on GitHub.

### 2. The Trials of Satya Nadella — *Newcomer*
Microsoft's AI renaissance narrative is cracking. Stock is down 19% YTD — the worst Mag 7 performer — driven by a confluence of self-made problems. Mustafa Suleyman, who ran Microsoft's consumer AI, has been removed from product oversight after overseeing a Copilot with just 6 million daily active users (vs. ChatGPT's 440M and Gemini's 82M). Microsoft 365 Copilot has achieved only 15M paying seats against a 450M-seat commercial base; GitHub Copilot has lost its narrative first to Cursor, then to Claude Code. A wave of senior departures follows — Azure AI Platform head (to Anthropic), GitHub CEO, Xbox chief Phil Spencer, and EVP Rajesh Jha. Reporter Tom Dotan, who helped create the Nadella legend at the WSJ, argues the problem is structural: weak product design, questionable leadership selection, and organizational sprawl that a decade of good strategy couldn't fully repair.

### 3. OpenAI Acquires Astral (uv/ruff/ty) — *Simon Willison's Newsletter*
OpenAI has acquired Astral, the company behind uv (Python environment manager), ruff (linter/formatter), and ty (type checker). uv in particular is load-bearing infrastructure — downloaded 126 million times last month, just two years after launch, it has become the default answer to Python's legendary environment chaos. The Astral team joins OpenAI's Codex team. Simon Willison notes a subtle framing gap: Astral emphasises staying open source and continuing community development, while OpenAI's announcement focuses on accelerating Codex. The Python open source community had already been uneasy about VC-backed control of uv; OpenAI ownership is a step further. There's also a talent angle — Astral has elite Rust engineers whose skills directly apply to the Rust-built Codex CLI. Also released this week: GPT-5.4 mini and GPT-5.4 nano, which can describe 76,000 photos for $52.

### 4. State of the Product Job Market in Early 2026 — *Lenny's Newsletter*
The most optimistic of Lenny's four biannual reports. PM openings are at a 3-year high (7,300+ globally, up 75% from the 2023 trough and 20% since January). Engineering openings stand at 67,000 globally, with no slowdown attributed to AI. AI-specific roles are hockey-sticking — comprising all positions at AI companies plus AI-specific titles at non-AI companies. The surprise finding: design roles have been completely flat since early 2023, which Lenny hypothesises is AI-driven (engineers moving faster, less appetite for traditional design process). The Bay Area now holds 23% of all open PM roles (up 50% since 2022) and a third of all AI roles. Remote opportunities continue declining. NYC has cemented itself as the #2 global tech hub. Data from TrueUp, tracking 9,000+ companies.

### 5. How Netflix Live Streams to 100 Million Devices in 60 Seconds — *ByteByteGo*
A deep system design look at Netflix's Live Origin — the custom server sitting between their cloud encoding pipelines and the Open Connect CDN. Netflix runs two independent encoding pipelines in different cloud regions simultaneously; the Live Origin picks the best segment from whichever pipeline produced a clean result. To handle storage at live scale, they moved from S3 to a combination of Apache Cassandra (for consistency and write availability even with a full availability zone outage) and EVCache (write-through distributed cache), dropping median latency from 113ms to 25ms and enabling the system to absorb 200+ Gbps read surges without degrading writes. During the Tyson vs. Paul fight in 2024, the system handled a historic peak of 65 million concurrent streams. Priority-based rate limiting ensures live-edge traffic is always served before DVR traffic during load spikes.

### 6. Why Is OpenAI Launching a Super App? — *Prof G Markets*
OpenAI is merging ChatGPT, its Atlas browser, and Codex into a single desktop "super app," a direct response to slipping market position. Anthropic now captures over 70% of first-time B2B AI spending — up sharply from OpenAI's 60% share as recently as December 2025. Galloway's read: OpenAI became "the AI brand people fear" — antihuman and disingenuous — while Anthropic's public feud with the Pentagon and careful brand positioning made it more trusted. The piece doubles as a framework for when side bets work: you need money (core business cash flow), infrastructure leverage (do existing assets compound the bet?), and a genuinely good idea. Galloway praises Altman for having the backbone to kill what isn't working — citing sunk cost clarity as the most underrated CEO skill.

## Trending

1. **AI lab consolidation and platform wars** — OpenAI acquires Astral (Python tooling), launches super app, loses B2B share to Anthropic. Microsoft's Copilot struggles as talent exits. The AI tooling and distribution layer is becoming the real battleground.

2. **AI tools as cognitive load reducers** — The Montaigne agent story crystallises a maturing theme: agents are most valuable not because they go fast, but because they preserve human energy for high-judgment decisions. Knowledge encoding (skills, business context, data definitions) is the hard, valuable work.

3. **Tech job market bifurcation** — AI roles are hockey-sticking while design roles flatline. PM and engineering openings are near multi-year highs. The labour market is not collapsing — it's splitting into AI-adjacent (growing fast) and AI-adjacent-but-exposed (flat or declining).
