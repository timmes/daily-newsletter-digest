## Top Stories

### 1. Salesforce Is Betting Everything on Agents — And Ignoring the UI
**Source:** [Engineering Leadership](https://newsletter.eng-leadership.com/) · Gregor Ojstersek · Apr 27

At Salesforce TDX 2026, Gregor Ojstersek observed a company in full agentic pivot: "Everything on Salesforce is now an API, MCP tool, or CLI" — what they're calling Headless 360. The traditional UI is becoming optional plumbing, with Agentforce Vibes 2.0 (a VS Code extension) as their developer bet. The most substantive concept is the distinction between AGI and EGI (Enterprise General Intelligence): Salesforce's argument that enterprise AI doesn't need human-level general intelligence, just enough to reliably act on business workflows. Three techniques from Salesforce AI Research point toward where agentic enterprise software is heading: simulation environments for agent training (agents learning from realistic enterprise scenarios), synthetic data for privacy-safe testing, and AI Evals as a first-class engineering practice. Salesforce also open-sourced Agent Script — a language for defining agent behavior — and announced ADLC (Agent Development Lifecycle) skills for Claude Code. Whether EGI becomes a meaningful category or marketing noise, the structural shift is real: Salesforce is designing for a world where agents are the primary interface, and the browser UI is an afterthought.

### 2. AI Token Spending Is Out of Control — And Nobody Is Slowing Down
**Source:** [The Pragmatic Engineer](https://newsletter.pragmaticengineer.com/) · Gergely Orosz · Apr 23

A 15-company survey puts hard numbers on what many suspected: AI token spending has exploded 10-15x in six months, with some developers running up $500/day on Claude Code. The kicker is the math — when you add token costs to salary, the effective cost of a developer has nearly doubled at heavy-use companies. Company responses vary: cheaper model defaults, usage routing (Claude Haiku for non-critical tasks), and real-time monitoring dashboards — but there is no evidence of actual slowdown. Two additional signals stand out: Anthropic and GitHub Copilot are now throttling individual users to prioritize business accounts, while OpenAI/Codex is doing the opposite, giving individuals more headroom as a market-share tactic. Meta morale is at an all-time low, driven by layoff anxiety and an invasive employee tracking program. The data point that lingers: a few teams have quietly doubled their engineering budgets through AI usage costs, with no commensurate productivity gain visible at the org level yet.

### 4. You Are the Most Expensive Model
**Source:** [Every](https://every.to/) · Mike Taylor · Apr 27

Mike Taylor's framework is deceptively simple: when you're supervising AI agents, *you* are the most expensive model in the loop. The cost of your time — reviewing, correcting, redirecting — frequently exceeds the API spend you're trying to optimize. His four-step approach: (1) map every human intervention point in your agent workflow, (2) estimate the real time cost per review, not just the API cost, (3) ask whether each intervention is improving output quality or just creating the feeling of control, (4) identify which interventions you can eliminate by improving prompts, context, or agent design upstream. The implication for engineering leaders is that "keep a human in the loop" is not a free safety net — it is a cost center that needs to be measured and optimized like any other. Teams treating human review as zero-cost will systematically underestimate their true AI operating costs and wonder why the economics never quite work out.
