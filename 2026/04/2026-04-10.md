## Top Stories

### 1. How We Run a 25-person Company on Four AI Agents
**Source:** Every (Source Code) · Katie Parrott

Every runs six products, a media company, and a consultancy with 25 people — and they've built four custom Notion AI agents that replace a significant amount of manual coordination overhead. The four agents are: **Anton** (the prioritization agent, which answers "What should I work on today?" for any team member and posts daily priority summaries to Slack, replacing a human COO who used to manually cross-reference calendars and task lists); **Max** (the meetings-to-tasks agent, which processes transcripts and posts numbered action items to Slack — team members reply with which numbers to create as tasks, and Max does it automatically); the **Strategy Interviewer** (an OKR agent that interviews each team member about quarterly goals and produces structured OKRs aligned to company strategy — Every's entire team completed their Q2 OKRs in two days this way); and the **Campaign Reporter** (a daily growth scorecard posted to Slack pulling from PostHog and Stripe via Notion Workers). The key insight: all four agents share the same three interconnected databases — a calendar, a tasks database, and a people database — making each new agent incrementally cheaper to build. The team's biggest productivity unlock wasn't AI speed; it was removing the human-in-the-middle coordination layer entirely.

### 2. Meta Created an Internal Leaderboard on AI Token Usage
**Source:** Engineering Leadership · Gregor Ojstersek

Meta built "Claudeonomics" — an internal leaderboard ranking 85,000+ employees by AI token consumption, a bottom-up initiative built by engineers and shared on the company intranet. In the past 30 days, total usage exceeded 60 trillion tokens at an estimated cost of $900 million; the top individual user consumed 281 billion tokens. Employees earn badges (bronze through emerald) and the top 250 are dubbed "power users" with titles like "Session Immortal" and "Token Legend." This is part of a broader Silicon Valley "tokenmaxxing" trend: Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang says he'd be "very concerned" if a $500k engineer spent less than $250k on AI tokens annually; Databricks' CEO highlighted a $7,000/two-week token user as a role model; Meta CTO Andrew Bosworth claims engineers who spend their salary-equivalent on AI tokens see up to 10x productivity gains. However, employees are already gaming the system by letting agents run continuously to maximize token count — and critics compare the metric to measuring lines of code. The real question Gregor raises: are these companies measuring genuine productivity gains, or manufacturing the appearance of AI adoption?

### 3. Developer Ramp-Up Time Continues to Accelerate with AI
**Source:** Engineering Enablement · Justin Reock

Analysis of 400 companies (500+ developers each, Oct 2025–Feb 2026) shows that Time to 10th PR — the standard measure of new developer onboarding speed — now averages **33 days**, down from 39 days in Q4 2025. That's a further 15% quarter-over-quarter reduction, and more than a 50% reduction since Q1 2024 (when the baseline was 91 days for daily AI users). The driver isn't individual AI adoption choices anymore — in many organizations, AI is now mandatory for onboarding: developers are guided to use AI to ask questions about codebases, understand architectural decisions, and draft initial PRs for senior review. The "figuring things out" phase has been compressed dramatically. Important caveats: Time to 10th PR is a narrow metric that says nothing about code quality, rework rates, or depth of understanding. Whether we're approaching a natural floor (tasks that simply can't be AI-compressed, like relationship-building and judgment acquisition) remains an open question. ## Trending

1. **AI tools for structured thinking and productivity** — Every's multi-agent org and the Meta token leaderboard both reflect the same underlying dynamic: companies are systematically restructuring coordination and accountability around AI agents rather than treating AI as individual productivity tools. The question is shifting from "how much did AI help?" to "who's in the loop, and when?"

2. **Engineering leadership and career growth** — Two separate newsletters today (Level Up's EQ piece, Engineering Leadership's tokenmaxxing analysis) converge on the same point: technical implementation skill is being commoditized, and what matters is judgment about *where* to apply AI, not *how* to run it.

3. **AI coding agents at enterprise scale** — Developer ramp-up time falling 50% in 18 months is the clearest quantitative signal yet that AI is restructuring the economics of engineering team growth. The bottleneck is no longer writing or even merging code — it's reviewing it.
